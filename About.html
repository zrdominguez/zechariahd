My History

I didn’t start out knowing I’d be a software engineer. My path wasn’t linear, and honestly, it took some time before I realized where my passion truly lay. But looking back, the signs were always there. I have always been fascinated by how things worked and how I could make them work better. Between tweaking a gadget to fit my needs or completely reimagining its purpose, I loved the challenge of taking something apart and rebuilding it in my own way.

 Resume

I i nstalled a mod chip into a PlayStation 1 motherboard. I wasn’t just following a tutorial, I was experimenting, learning the delicate process of soldering, and figuring out how to push hardware beyond its original limitations. The thrill of making an old console do something it was never designed to do opened my eyes to the endless possibilities of engineering.

I Then there was my Raspberry Pi arcade cabinet project. Which was a deep dive into custom hardware and software. I wanted to build the entire arcade experience from scratch. Converting a Raspberry Pi into the brain of a fully functional arcade cabinet required a mix of electrical work, programming, and tons of problem solving. It was the perfect blend of creativity and logic, and it reinforced my need to want to build it, shape it, and push it further.

With a degree in Integrated Studies, I took my curiosity to the next level, diving into game development and later enrolling in a coding bootcamp to sharpen my expertise. When the pandemic hit, I stepped up to support my family’s business, managing technical operations across eight McDonald’s locations. Troubleshooting hardware and software issues in a fast -paced, high-stakes environment taught me resilience and the importance of building systems that work. Today, I bring that same mindset to software engineering. Passion isn’t always obvious at first but it’s something you uncover through curiosity, persistence, and the willingness to take things apart just to understand how they work. And once you find it, there’s no limit to what you can build.

Projects

LangxChange

LangXchange is a web application designed to help language learners discover, review, and share insights about books in different languages. By offering user-generated reviews, LangXchange provides valuable information about books' grammar complexity, fluency level, and entertainment value, making it easier for learners to find the right resources for their language journey. The platform encourages a collaborative community of learners who can contribute their experiences and benefit from others' recommendations.

BopStop

BopStop is an e-commerce web application dedicated to supporting artists by offering a platform to sell their unique products. From merchandise and artwork to music and collectibles, BopStop connects fans with authentic, artist-created items. With a sleek design and seamless shopping experience, the app empowers artists to reach their audience while giving fans a direct way to support their favorite creators.

JohnWickBnB

JohnWickBnB is a mock Airbnb-inspired web application that offers users the ability to browse, book, and manage unique property listings. Designed with a sleek, modern interface, the platform provides features like interactive property details, booking management, and personalized user dashboards. Built as a full-stack project, JohnWickBnB showcases efficient backend management and a responsive frontend, delivering a smooth and intuitive experience.

Unity Space Shooter

Unity Space Shooter is an action-packed game built with Unity, featuring thrilling space combat and engaging gameplay mechanics. Players navigate a dynamic space environment, battling waves of enemies, collecting power-ups, and striving for high scores. With responsive controls, immersive particle effects, and a user-friendly interface, Unity Space Shooter delivers an exciting gaming experience that highlights creativity and technical game development skills.
